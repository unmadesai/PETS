Our Achievements are only due to the constant help and support of our staff, volunteers, donors and the people of Mumbai. Thank you. We hope to still go a long way!

Since its creation, PETS has always been in the forefront whenever it has come to animal rescue. So, it is a matter of great pride that PETS has managed to rehabilitate more than 1000 stray dogs by January 2015. PETS thanks all volunteers who have supported and helped in animal rescue and are the reason for many stray dogs being alive even today.

The People for the Ethical Treatment of Strays NGO was awarded the "Most Promising NGO" award by the Government of Maharashtra, after we managed to raise more than Rs. 10 lakh towards funding to build a new animal welfare and healthcare centre in Ghatkopar. The newly built centre was unveiled in 2012 and continues to function as an animal rescue NGO in central Mumbai.

The PETS Society was awarded the "Animal BFF 2010" award by the Times of India as a part of their Animal Welfare Awareness and Rescue Drive (AWARD), for rescuing and sheltering over 500 stray dogs and also raising awareness for their adoption and fostering.

PETS managed to cross over 100 full-time staff, all of whom are dedicated towards animal welfare and rescue. We are also proud to announce the PETS Society has over 200 volunteers across Mumbai, who are the driving force behind all our campaigns and drives. We hope to keep this family growing over the years, to ensure better animal welfare and rescue activities across not only Mumbai but also other cities.

In 2007, PETS won the "AWA NGO of the Year" award, presented by the Animal Welfare Association (AWA) of India, for its efforts towards raising awareness about the benefits of adopting an Indian Stray Dog versus a Pedigree alternative. The award is one of the most prestigious awards, and PETS is proud to have been acknowledged for its efforts.
